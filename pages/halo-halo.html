Halo-Halo

Desciption

Halo-halo is a Filipino-style shaved ice made with sweetened beans, fruits, and jellies and topped with milk, Leche flan, purple yam jam, and ice cream. Halo-halo translates to "mix-mix," which is exactly what you do with all the ingredients. While this recipe will only have some of the ingredients that you can add to Halo-Halo, the dessert is customizable and you can add and remove ingredients as you see fit.

Ingredients (for 4 servings)

  • 1/2 cup gulaman cubes/sago
  • 1/2 cup sweetened beans (garbanzo, red mung, and/or white beans)
  • 1/2 cup nata de coco, drained well
  • 1/2 cup kaong, drained well
  • 1/2 cup sweetened saba bananas
  • 1/2 cup sweetened jackfruit strips
  • 1/4 cup sweetened macapuno
  • 1/2 cup ube halaya
  • Shaved ice
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1 cup leche flan
  • 4 scoops ice cream (mango, ube, or vanilla)
  • Potential Other Ingredients:
    • Sweet corn kernels
    • Rice crispies
    • Anything else you think would go well with the sweet flavor/texture

Steps

  1. Prepare the gulaman according to package directions. Use less water than called for to yield a firmer texture.
  2. Prepare the ingredients from scratch or use bottled Halo-Halo ingredients.
  3. In a tall glass, add about a teaspoon each of desired add-ins to about 1/3 full.
  4. Pack the glass with shaved ice.
  5. Drizzle evaporated milk over the ice.
  6. Top the ice with leche flan and scoop of ice cream.
  7. Garnish with strips of macapuno and sprinkle with pinipig, if desired.
  8. Mix the ingredients if you'd like. You've just made Halo-Halo!
